Our ABL Program

Activity-Based Learning (ABL) Program

The initiative focuses on equipping government schools with Activity-Based Learning (ABL) Kits and Art Kits to foster creativity, critical thinking, and holistic development among students from Classes I to V. The program is designed to support students by providing them with tools that enhance their learning experience in literacy, numeracy, spatial understanding, motor skills, and more.

The ABL Kits are specifically tailored for Class 1-2 and Class 3-5, ensuring age-appropriate support for early education. These kits include a variety of tools such as lacing buttons, 2D shapes, pattern blocks, abacuses, and more, all aimed at interactive, hands-on learning experiences. In addition, Art Kits are being distributed to over 4 million students, containing essential supplies like pencils, sharpeners, erasers, sketchbooks, wax crayons, and drawing books to encourage creativity and artistic expression.

Key Highlights of the ABL Program

The ABL program is designed to provide educators and students with the necessary tools and resources to:

Equip schools with tailored ABL and Art Kits for enhanced learning

Enable interactive learning for Classes I to V with hands-on tools

Promote creativity and artistic expression among young learners

Implement a robust monitoring framework to track program success

This program aims to transform classrooms into dynamic learning environments, where students engage in interactive, practical activities that promote holistic development. With a strong focus on creativity and skill-building, the ABL program is set to lay a solid foundation for the future success of young learners.
